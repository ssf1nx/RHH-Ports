# Work With Me

I make software run where it isn't meant to. Most of my public work is porting PC and indie games to Linux ARM handhelds — weak hardware, tight memory, GLES instead of full OpenGL, no vendor support — but that's just where the skill shows up. The actual work is getting things to build, run, and behave on awkward targets, and figuring out why they don't.

My ports and guides are free, and they stay free — that's community work, and I'm not putting a price on the public stuff. This page is about something separate: private, dedicated work for commercial projects. If you're a studio or publisher who needs your own game handled on your schedule and to your spec, that's contract work, and that's what I'm available for.

Plenty of capable people in this scene will tell you, for free, whether a game can be ported. That's not what I'm selling. What I do is the part after that: work out *why* it doesn't run, figure out the path to making it, and then actually do it — get it built, optimized, and shipped on the target. The diagnosis is the easy half and it's everywhere. The detailed work of making something feasible and then making it real is what I'm hired for.

I take on select freelance and contract work. I prefer scoped, asynchronous projects over open-ended commitments or on-call arrangements.

## What I do

- **Cross-platform porting.** Getting games and software running natively on Linux, Steam Deck, ARM, and Android, including the wrapper, translation, and runtime work that involves (GameMaker, Godot, Wine/box64, GLES).
- **QA and platform testing.** Structured, reproducible bug reports across builds, regression testing, and catching the crashes and performance problems that only surface on real low-end hardware — before they reach players or storefront review.
- **Build and release engineering.** CI, cross-compilation, runtime bundling, and installers.
- **Getting stalled things moving.** Legacy or decompiled codebases that won't build on modern toolchains, or are stuck partway. I work out what's broken and get it running again, and I write down what I did.

## How I work

I use AI as an active part of my process, and I use it carefully. It's a fast implementer, not a replacement for understanding the system. I write specific prompts, verify what comes back, and know when not to lean on it. What you get is work I can explain and stand behind.

I'm an integrator and a debugger more than a from-scratch engine author. My strength is understanding how something is supposed to work, finding where it breaks, and getting it fixed. I'll tell you plainly when a job is outside what I do well.
